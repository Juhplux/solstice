Solstice is a Coding Party

Solstice is a social coding event open to all middle and high school students from the Boys and Girls Club of Lake County. We'll come together for a day to discover the joy of code, build creative projects, and share them with the world!

Solstice is organized in public by teenagers just like yourself at Hack Club - and we want you to contribute! Join us in the #chicago-day-of-service channel on the Hack Club Slack to help craft this event into something truly magical.

Workshops

Participants create their own Printed Circuit Board (PCB) keychain that they can design and customize themselves, as well as connect to any website of their choice and has an LED function. Through this workshop, Scouts will learn how to use EasyEDA, a circuit design software, and the fundamentals of PCB design using the EDA website.

Participants will be able to build a Choose Your Own Adventure Game (CYOA) that incorporates music, interactive elements, and the ability to control all stylistic features. At the end they will have a playable game built with Python fit to share with family, and friends, and publish.

Participants will create their own pixel art based video game with the JavaScript web-based editor Sprig. Through this track, attendees will learn about storytelling, designing assets, and designing the functionality of their game.

FAQs

How much does it cost?

Solstice is entirely free! Everything from snacks to dinner to the incredible swag we'll get is covered by Hack Club.

I have no clue how to code. Can I still come?

Yes! Solstice is beginner-friendly, and we'll be holding workshops to get you started building your first coding projects.

Where/When is Solstice?

Solstice is being held from 9am-4pm on Friday, June 21 at Abott Middle School (949 Van St, Elgin, IL 60123)!

What do I need to bring?

You'll just need your laptops (school computers work)! There will be tablets with keyboards available if you don't have a laptop.

I have another question!

Feel free to reach out! You can either send a message in the #chicago-day-of-service channel in the Hack Club Slack or send an email to nila@hackclub.com with any questions you may have.
